Dataset Overview & Analysis Summary

Data Source

Dataset: YouTube Video Analytics Dataset (600+ videos)
Source: Kaggle - YouTube Video View Prediction EDA & ML
Original Size: 600 videos across multiple YouTube channels and categories

Dataset Description

The dataset contains comprehensive YouTube video metadata including video performance metrics, publishing details, and content characteristics. Each record represents a unique YouTube video with 13 key attributes covering engagement metrics (views, likes, comments), temporal information (publish date/time), content metadata (title, description, tags, category), and technical details (duration, thumbnails). The data spans multiple content categories from gaming and entertainment to education and technology, providing a diverse sample for analysis.

Analysis & Preprocessing Pipeline

Data Cleaning

  • Removed 16 duplicate records (600 ‚Üí 584 videos)
  • Handled timezone-aware datetime parsing
  • Cleaned and standardized tag formatting

Feature Engineering

  • Created temporal features (hour, day, weekend flags)
  • Extracted title characteristics (length, word count)
  • Calculated engagement rates (like/view, comment/view)
  • Developed performance tier classifications

Outlier Treatment

  • Maintained data integrity while reducing noise

Machine Learning

XGBoost came second at 77.9%, followed by Gradient Boosting at 72.5%. The traditional linear models (Ridge, Lasso, and Linear Regression) all performed similarly around 71.5%. This ranking shows that ensemble methods capture YouTube's complex engagement patterns significantly better than simple linear relationships.

Performance by Publishing Hour

‚è∞ Interpretation: Peak engagement occurs at 2 PM (14:00) UTC with 8.5M average views, followed by 4 PM at 8.2M. Early morning hours (0-6 AM) show the lowest performance. This suggests audiences are most active during afternoon hours, making 2-4 PM the optimal publishing window for maximum reach.

Views by Day of Week

üìÖ Interpretation: Thursday leads with 9.5M average views, followed by Tuesday (9.2M) and Wednesday (8.8M). Weekends show lower engagement - Saturday (6.5M) and Sunday (7.2M). This indicates that mid-week publishing captures audience attention when they're most engaged with content during work/school days.

Feature Importance

üéØ Interpretation: Like count dominates as the strongest predictor (100%), followed by comments (85%) and duration (70%). This reveals that engagement metrics are more important than technical factors for view prediction. Content creators should prioritize creating likeable, discussion-worthy content over optimizing technical aspects.
